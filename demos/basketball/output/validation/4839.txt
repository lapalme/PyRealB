The Houston Rockets defeated the host Golden State Warriors, 122-121, at Oracle Arena on
Tuesday. In the opening game of the season for these title contenders, it came down to the
final seconds to decide a winner. Despite the fact that the Warriors led for over 46
minutes of this game, the Rockets ended up prevailing in comeback fashion. The win has to
be attributed to a monster fourth quarter, in which Houston outscored Golden State, 34-20.
Turnovers really killed the Warriors, as they committed 17 of them in their defeat.
Houston also did a fantastic job down low, outscoring Golden State, 54-32, in the paint.
The Rockets (10) were huge underdogs in this game, but James Harden carried them to an
impressive win. Harden led Houston with 27 points, six rebounds and 10 assists. Eric
Gordon led the bench in scoring, providing 24 points, despite going 0-of-6 from three-
point range. P.J. Tucker was also huge off the bench, totaling 20 points and six rebounds.
Chris Paul went just 2-of-9 from the field in his debut with Houston, but still finished
with four points, eight rebounds, 11 assists and two steals. The Warriors (0-1) are
prohibited favorites to repeat on their title this year, but a loss like this is
uncharacteristic of this bunch. Draymond Green was the difference, as he tallied nine
points, 11 rebounds and 13 assists before departing in the third quarter due to a knee
injury. Once he went out, the Rockets overcame a double-digit deficit to comeback and win.
Nick Young led the Warriors in scoring, as he dropped 23 points on 8-of-9 shooting off the
bench. Kevin Durant amassed 20 points, five rebounds, seven assists and four blocks,
despite committing nine turnovers. Stephen Curry accrued 22 points, five rebounds and four
