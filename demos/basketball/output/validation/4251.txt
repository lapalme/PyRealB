*** Reference summary
The Los Angeles Lakers took down a shorthanded Oklahoma City Thunder, 106-81, at Staples
Center on Thursday. With ankle injuries keeping reigning MVP Russell Westbrook and veteran
forward Carmelo Anthony out of the lineup, offense was hard to come by for the Thunder
(31-25). This was particularly pronounced in the second half, as OKC scored only 35 points
over the third and fourth quarter. The Thunder had an unusually inefficient night,
shooting just 36 percent from the floor in the absence of their two stars. The Lakers
(23-31), meanwhile, came out strong in the second half and scored 31 third-quarter points.
The team had five double-digit scorers in the win and knocked down 10 three-pointers. The
25-point win marked their largest margin of victory this season. This was all despite the
team being shorthanded following a deadline-day trade. Los Angeles agreed to ship Jordan
Clarkson and Larry Nance, Jr. to Cleveland in exchange for Channing Frye, a first-round
pick, and former Boston Celtics star Isaiah Thomas. Neither of the Lakers' new additions
were able to join the team for Thursday's win, but the team earned the easy win
nonetheless. LA's top scorer was Kentavious Caldwell-Pope, who had 20 points on 7-of-10
shooting. Brandon Ingram scored 19 points and dished out six assists. Julius Randle, whose
name was tossed around in trade rumors before the deadline, scored another 17. For OKC,
All-Star forward Paul George had a green light with Westbrook and Anthony absent. George
scored a team-high 29 points and attempted 25 shots to get there. While the team missed
the majority of its shots, center Steven Adams did provide them with plenty of second
chances. He had seven offensive rebounds on the night. The Lakers will visit Dallas on
Saturday, while OKC will host the Memphis Grizzlies on Sunday.
