*** Reference summary
The Golden State Warriors took down the Atlanta Hawks, 103-94, at Oracle Arena on Friday
night. The win came with a heavy price tag for Golden State (54-18), as superstar point
guard Steph Curry exited the game early with an apparent MCL injury. The two-time MVP
limped to the locker room after colliding with center JaVale McGee. Friday was Curry's
first game back after missing the last two weeks with an ankle sprain. He will undergo an
MRI on Saturday to determine the severity of the injury. Despite Curry's absence in the
second half, the Warriors managed to overtake the Hawks (21-52), who had led for the
majority of the game. Golden State was also missing All-Stars Klay Thompson (hand), Kevin
Durant (rib), and Draymond Green (pelvic contusion). Still, the Warriors enjoyed a potent
offensive night. The team knocked down 12 three-pointers and dished out 27 total assists.
Curry was still the team's leader with 29 points. Veteran guard Nick Young, meanwhile,
scored 24 points while shooting 6-of-11 from three-point range. Atlanta, on the other
hand, shot just 44 percent from the field. Sophomore forward Taurean Prince led the team
with 20 points, but shot only 6-of-18 on the night. Dennis Schroder, meanwhile, scored 16
points to go with seven assists. Schroder returned to the lineup on Friday after missing
Thursday night's game for rest. The Hawks will visit Houston on Sunday, while Golden State
will host the Utah Jazz.
