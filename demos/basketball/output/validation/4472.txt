*** Reference summary
The Orlando Magic defeated the host New Orleans Pelicans, 115-99, at Smoothie King Center
on Monday evening. The Magic (5-2) led for most of the first half, but the Pelicans (3-4)
turned it up late and took a 64-60 lead going into halftime. The teams exchanged runs in
the third quarter, though the Magic ended up emerging with an 88-84 lead heading into the
final frame. The Magic dominated the fourth quarter, outscoring the Pelicans 28-15 to
cruise to the win. The Magic continue to enjoy success thanks to a balanced attack. On
Monday, five players scored in double digits, including three guys with 20 or more points.
Nikola Vucevic was the scoring leader for Orlando, with 21 points on 9-of-17 shooting,
along with eight rebounds and three assists in 27 minutes. Evan Fournier and Jonathan
Simmons chipped in 20 points each, while Marreese Speights exploded for 18 points on
6-of-11 shooting in 21 minutes off the bench. Meanwhile, Anthony Davis was in a world of
his own as he totaled 39 points on 13-of-20 shooting, 10 rebounds and four blocks in 41
minutes. DeMarcus Cousins was relatively quiet with only nine points to go along with 12
rebounds, five assists, one block and one steal in 39 minutes. Jrue Holiday was the only
player other than Davis to score in double digits, finishing with 11 points, seven assists
and four rebounds in 36 minutes. The Pelicans will look to bounce back as they play host
to the Timberwolves on Wednesday. The Magic take on the Grizzlies in Memphis on Wednesday.
