The Detroit Pistons defeated the host Chicago Bulls, 119-87, at United Center on
Wednesday. In a much-needed finale for both of these teams, Detroit won in dominating
fashion. A 35-21 first quarter really set the tone, as Chicago never got the game within
an eight-point margin from there. In addition, they led by at least 20 points for the
entirety of the fourth quarter. The shooting disparity was drastic, with Detroit
connecting on 53 percent of their shots while Chicago hit just 37 percent from the field.
Defense was also key, with the Pistons recording 12 steals and four blocks while the Bulls
had just eight steals and one block. That led to Detroit winning the turnover
differential, 18-11. The Pistons (39-43) were led by Luke Kennard, as he tallied 23
points, five rebounds and five assists. Eric Moreland started and accumulated 16 points,
17 rebounds, four assists, four steals and four blocks. Henry Ellenson led the bench with
14 points and 10 boards. The Bulls (27-55) were led by Lauri Markkanen, as he accrued 20
points, eight rebounds and two steals. Sean Kilpatrick totaled 14 points, two rebounds and
four assists. David Nwaba generated 13 points, six rebounds and two assists. Neither of
these clubs reached the postseason, as Detroit finished ninth in the Eastern Conference
