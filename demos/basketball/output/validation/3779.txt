The Miami Heat defeated the host Chicago Bulls, 100-93, at United Center on Sunday
afternoon. After a deplorable first quarter, the Bulls led 13-7, as both teams combined to
go 6-of-43 from the field. Things picked up in the second, where they Heat went off for 38
points in the quarter, to take a 45-42 lead into halftime. They maintained their lead,
taking a 64-61 advantage into the fourth quarter. Miami took to a 16-5 run midway through
the quarter, however, it was still a five-point game with under four minutes to go. In the
end, some clutch shooting by Goran Dragic down the stretch ensured their third straight
win. Goran Dragic led the way for the Heat, with 24 points, three assists and two
rebounds, in 33 minutes. Wayne Ellington continued his hot play, with 19 points on 5-of-8
shooting, in 29 minutes off the bench. James Johnson filled the stat sheet, as he poured
in 15 points, seven rebounds, six assists, two steals and two blocks, in 28 minutes off
the bench. The Heat shot 45 percent from the field in the contest, including 13-of-32 from
long range. Meanwhile, Denzel Valentine was close to a triple double, as he finished with
14 points, a career-high 13 rebounds and a career-high seven assists, in 39 minutes.
Jerian Grant poured in 24 points on 8-of-16 shooting, in 30 minutes off the bench. Lauri
Markkanen followed up with 11 points and 10 rebounds, while Robin Lopez chipped in 10
points, seven rebounds and three assists, in 27 minutes. The Bulls shot 37 percent from
the field, including 14-of-33 from long range. This marks the fifth straight loss for
Chicago. The Bulls will look to get back on track as they play host to the Suns on
Tuesday. The Heat take on the Cavaliers in Cleveland on Tuesday.
