*** Reference summary
The Oklahoma City Thunder defeated the Atlanta Hawks, 119-107, at Philips Arena on Tuesday
evening. The Thunder (41-29) came into the contest with a markedly better record and
prospectus than the Hawks (20-48), but with a reputation for struggling against inferior
opponents from time to time as well, especially on the road. Like clockwork, that
deficiency seemed to rear its ugly head in the first half, as Atlanta forged an eye-
opening 66-54 halftime advantage. That seemed to be a wake-up call for the visitors, which
bounced back to not only erase the entirety of their 12-point deficit, but actually garner
a slim two-point advantage heading into the final 12 minutes. However, OKC simply couldn't
shake their feisty hosts for more than the first half of the fourth quarter, and the game
stood knotted at 103-103 with 5:18 remaining following a Taurean Prince 25-foot three-
pointer. That's when the Thunder finally exerted their will, rattling off a jaw-dropping
160 run over the ensuing 4:24 that put the contest out of reach. They were able to prevail
without some key personnel, ast starting center Steven Adams (hip) missed the contest and
Paul George suffered an in-game groin injury. Russell Westbrook paced the Thunder with a
32-point, 12-rebound, 12-assist triple-double-- the 100th of his career-- and also
included one steal and one block. Carmelo Anthony followed with 21 points, four rebounds
and three blocks. Jerian Grant managed 20 points, five boards, one assist, two steals and
two blocks. George contributed 12 points, four rebounds, three assists and one steal in 26
minutes before exiting. Patrick Patterson matched George's scoring total off the bench and
complemented it with two rebounds and one block. Corey Brewer drew another start at
shooting guard and mustered seven points, four assists, two rebounds, three steals and two
blocks. Dakari Johnson drew the start for Adams but only managed three points and two
boards. Prince led the way for the Hawks with 25 points, a total he supplemented with
eight boards, three assists, two blocks and one steal. Dennis Schroder generated 18
points, eight assists and two boards. Damion Lee paced the second unit with 13 points and
also hauled in four rebounds, dished out two assists and recorded one steal. Isaiah Taylor
was right behind him with 12 points, which he complemented with four assists, three
rebounds and one steal. Andrew White III was productive off the bench with nine points and
five rebounds across 20 minutes. Dewayne Dedmon (five points, six rebounds, one assist and
two steals) and Tyler Dorsey (five points, two assists, one rebound) made modest
contributions as starters. The Thunder head back home to engage in a Friday night showdown
with the Clippers, while the Hawks remain home to tangle with the Charlotte Hornets on
Thursday evening.
