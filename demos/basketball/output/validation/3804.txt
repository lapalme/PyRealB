The Boston Celtics defeated the Chicago Bulls 105-89 on Monday at United Center.
Surprisingly, the final score of Monday's contest suggests that things were closer than
they actually were. The Celtics (45-20) entered the fourth quarter with a 33-point lead
without star point guard Kyrie Irving (knee soreness) thanks to an efficient effort from
second-year standout Jaylen Brown, who scored a game-high 21 points on 9-of-13 shooting in
just 28 minutes. Four other Celtics managed to score in double figures, with Jayson Tatym
adding 14 in 20 minutes, Al Horford posting 13 points and seven rebounds in 22 minutes and
Terry Rozier and Greg Monroe adding 13 and 10 respectively off the bench. Monday's win
wasn't about the offensive production for Boston, however, as their defense was stout as
it has been all season. The Bulls (21-42) managed to shoot just 40 percent from the field,
as the starting unit just couldn't get anything going offensively, with the five combining
to score just 40 points all together. Denzel Valentine, however, was very impressive off
the bench, scoring a team-high 20 points on 4-of-6 shooting from behind the arc and
7-of-11 fro the field overall in just 29 minutes. On the other side of the spectrum, Zach
LaVine had one of the worst shooting nights of his young NBA career, going 1-of-11 from
the field and finishing with just four points in 27 minutes.
