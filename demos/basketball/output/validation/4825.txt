*** Reference summary
The Portland Trail Blazers defeated the Golden State Warriors on Wednesday, 123-117,
behind Damian Lillard's 44 points at Moda Center. What started as a runaway night for the
Portland offense quickly turned into a shootout between two of the league's most potent
scorers. Portland's Damian Lillard got going early in the night, helping his team to a
40-point first quarter. Lillard kept his strong showing going, exchanging bigtime shots
with Golden State forward Kevin Durant. Thanks to a 50-point night from Durant, Golden
State clawed back to tie the game at 99-99 in the fourth quarter. The Warriors never took
the lead, though, as C.J. McCollum quickly converted an and-one and Lillard continued his
heroics to provide nine more points by the game's end. The Blazers (32-26) saw a combined
73 points from their backcourt duo of Damian Lillard and C.J. McCollum. Lillard was the
star of the show, going 14-of-25 from the field and 12-of-12 from the free-throw line en
route to 44 points. Big man Jusuf Nurkic also provided a much-needed interior presence in
the win. Nurkic finished with 17 points and 13 rebounds. Golden State (44-14) will enter
the All-Star Break without the league's best record for the first time since 2014, as the
team slipped behind Houston in the standings after Wednesday's loss. The loss was
particularly tough to swallow due to Kevin Durant's huge performance. Durant set a new
personal high for scoring in a Warriors uniform, dropping 50 points on 17-of-27 shooting
and 10-of-10 from the free-throw line. While Durant excelled, fellow former MVP Steph
Curry had a rare off night. Curry shot just 6-of-17 from the field and committed seven
turnovers. Both teams will be off through the All-Star Break.
