*** Reference summary
The Houston Rockets defeated the Los Angeles Lakers, 126-111, at Toyota Center on Thursday
evening. The Rockets (13-14) came into the contest trying to build on a Tuesday night win
against the Trail Blazers, which had snapped a three-game losing streak. However, they had
a formidable opponent in the red-hot Lakers (17-11), which had gone on a 15-5 run after
opening the season with a 2-5 record. Los Angeles unsurprisingly kept the pressure on
Houston throughout the majority of the game, taking a couple of fourth-quarter leads and
facing just a three-point deficit with 4:16 to play. However, James Harden, who led the
way with a 50-point, 11-assist, 10-rebound triple-double, took over from that point,
scoring 15 of his points during the remainder of the contest as the home squad finished on
a 17-5 run. LeBron James paced the Lakers with 29 points, adding five rebounds, four
assists and two steals. Kyle Kuzma provided 24 points, five assists, three rebounds, two
steals and one block. Lance Stephenson led the bench with 17 points. Josh Hart contributed
15 points, while JaVale McGee supplied 11 points, five rebounds, two blocks and one steal.
Clint Capela checked in a distant second behind Harden with a 16-point, 14-rebound double-
double that included one assist, two blocks and one steal. Danuel House, Jr. paced the
second unit with 15 points and also posted three rebounds and one steal. Chris Paul had a
near double-double with 14 points, nine assists, one rebound and three steals. Eric Gordon
drew a start for James Ennis (hamstring) and registered 13 points. The Lakers remain on
the road and visit the Charlotte Hornets on Saturday night, while the Rockets pay a visit
to the Memphis Grizzlies on Saturday evening as well.
