*** Reference summary
The San Antonio Spurs defeated the visiting Oklahoma City Thunder, 116-102, at AT & T
Center on Saturday evening. The Spurs got out to a great start at home, with a 38-25 lead
after one quarter. The Thunder picked it up in the second, but could only knock the
deficit down by four points, as they trailed by nine at halftime. The contest slowed down
in the third, as both teams combined for only 35 points, however, it was the Spurs who
played with the edge, as they outscored the visitors by five, to push their lead to 14,
entering the final frame. The Thunder kept fighting through the fourth but could not gain
any ground, as the Spurs held on for their second straight win. The Thunder dropped their
second in a row with out Paul George (shoulder) in the lineup, and their fourth straight
overall. LaMarcus Aldridge led the Spurs, with 27 points, 10 rebounds, one assist, one
steal and a block, in 34 minutes. Rudy Gay followed up with 22 points, eight rebounds, one
assists and one steal, in 26 minutes off the bench, while DeMar DeRozan added 18 points,
seven assists and six rebounds. The Spurs shot 50 percent from the field, including
8-of-19 from long range. Meanwhile, Russell Westbrook led the Thunder with 19 points, nine
rebounds, eight assists and one steal, in 33 minutes. Dennis Schroder followed up with 18
points, five rebounds, three assists and one steal, while Steven Adams added 17 points, 13
rebounds, two steals and a block. The Thunder shot 46 percent from the field, including
7-of-29 from deep. The Thunder (38-24) will look to bounce back as they play host to the
Grizzlies on Sunday. The Spurs (35-29) play host to the Nuggets on Monday.
