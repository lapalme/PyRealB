fourth quarter to take a 17-point lead with five minutes remaining. While it wasn't a
wire-to-wire win, Utah held the lead for the final three quarters. Rebounding was key for
the Jazz, as they outrebounded the Mavericks, 53-42. Free-throw shooting was also
critical, with Utah connecting on 29-of-33 and Dallas hitting just 14-of-18. The Mavericks
(26-33) had to play without Luka Doncic (ankle) yet again, as they were led by Tim
Hardaway Jr., who tallied 21 points, five rebounds and four assists. Dirk Nowitzki got a
rare start and totaled 15 points and two boards. Jalen Brunson accrued 13 points, five
rebounds and two assists. Trey Burke led the bench with 20 points, seven rebounds and
three assists. The Jazz (33-26) were led by Ricky Rubio, as he collected 25 points, four
rebounds and five assists. Donovan Mitchell accumulated 25 points, eight rebounds and six

finished with 15 points, 12 rebounds and three blocks. Jae Crowder led the bench with 22

Clippers on Monday. As for Utah, they're off until Wednesday, when they too play the
