The Boston Celtics defeated the New Orleans Pelicans, 124-107, at Smoothie King Center on
Monday It's been a tough stretch for the Celtics (11-10) here in late November, but they
managed to pick up a marquee win against one of the NBA's more talented teams after
dropping four of their last five games. And it wasn't just a win, it was a dominating win,
as Boston opened up a 13-point lead after the first quarter and never looked back. Kyrie
Irving led the way with a team-high 26 points on 10-of-22 shooting on top of 10 assists
and five steals in just 32 minutes. Al Horford and Jayson Tatum each dropped 20 points,
while Marcus Morris, who started his second straight game, posted a double-double of 19
points and 11 assists. The high scoring performances from the team's starters comes as a
result of the Celtics having a rare hot shooting night, as Boston shot a scorching
19-of-39 (49 percent) from behind the arc. The 124 points they scored Monday night was a
season-high for the team. As for the Pelicans (10-11), Monday's loss drops them to under
.500 for the first time in three weeks. It was also their fourth straight loss as the
team's odd season of streaks continues. Anthony Davis finished with a game-high 27 points
to go along with 16 rebounds, five assists, three steals and three blocks across 37
minutes. Nikola Mirotic was close behind, going 6-of-11 from behind the arc en route to 25
points of his own. But, while the starting frontcourt did its job, the starting backcourt
of Jrue Holiday and E'Twaun Moore flopped Monday night, combining for just 18 points on a
combined 6-of-15 shooting. What really hurt the Pelicans was their inability to take care
of the basketball, as they committed 22 turnovers to just 21 assists.
