The Denver Nuggets defeated the Memphis Grizzlies, 95-92, at FedExForum on Monday. With
Monday's contest being their third game in four days, the Nuggets (34-15) predictably came
out flat in Memphis. As a result, it took the the home team opened on an 18-4 run and
maintained that 14-point through the remainder of the first quarter. The Grizzlies (20-31)
ended up taking a 19-point lead into halftime and still maintained a 17-point advantage as
they entered the final period. And that's when everything went south, as the Nuggets
decided to wake up and put together a huge fourth quarter. After a Justin Holiday three-
pointer with just under 40 seconds left put the Grizzlies up one, All-Star Nikola Jokic
countered with a 7-foot jumper with 29 seconds left that would end up being the game-
winner and cap off a brilliant comeback effort. Jokic finished with a game-high 24 points
in 35 minutes, while Will Barton, who was back in the starting lineup due to the absence
of Jamal Murray (ankle), was close behind with 20 points on 9-of-15 shooting. The Nuggets'
bench as a whole didn't provide much, but Malik Beasley did have a quality night off the
bench with 18 points in 24 minutes. As for Memphis, only two players managed to score in
double figures in the disappointing loss, and it happened to be the team's two veteran
stars. Mark Gasol was dominant for most of Monday's game, scoring a game-high 28 points to
go along with nine rebounds in 38 minutes. Mike Conley came through with a double-double
of 23 points and 11 rebounds in 37 minutes, but unfortunately for the Grizzlies, the next
highest scorers were Kyle Anderson and Holiday with just nine points. On top of all that,
Memphis got just 15 points from its second unit Monday.
