*** Reference summary
The Los Angeles Clippers defeated the host Orlando Magic, 120-95, at Amway Center on
Friday. It's rare to see a home team dominated so thoroughly in the NBA but that was,
unfortunately, the case for the Magic here. The Clippers really made their mark in the
second quarter, outscoring Orlando, 35-22. The Magic never got the game within an eight-
point margin from there, as the Clippers led by at least 13 points for the entirety of the
fourth quarter. Three-point shooting was the difference in the game, with Los Angeles
connecting on 13-of-26 from long range while holding Orlando to just 4-of-17. Rebounding
was also huge, with the Clippers outrebounding the Magic, 50-40. Bench production was also
critical, with Los Angeles winning that battle, 65-35. The Clippers (5-4) got a vintage
performance from Lou Williams off the bench, as he provided 26 points, three rebounds and
three assists. Tobias Harris led the starters with 21 points, seven rebounds and two
assists. Boban Marjanovic got a rare start and accrued 10 points, 11 rebounds and two
assists. Montrezl Harrell also played well off the bench, totaling 10 points, 12 boards
and three blocks. The Magic (2-6) were led by Nikola Vucevic, as he tallied 22 points, 11
rebounds, three assists and three blocks. Evan Fournier finished with 19 points, three
rebounds, four assists and three steals. D.J. Augustin was the only other Orlando player
in double figures, as he collected 13 points, five rebounds and three assists. On deck for
Orlando is a road matchup versus the San Antonio Spurs on Sunday. As for Los Angeles,
they're off until Monday when they play host to the Minnesota Timberwolves.
