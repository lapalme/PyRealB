minor knee injury. Still, Milwaukee was able to pick up its 12 win in its last 13 games,
relying largely on a strong night from Eric Bledsoe. Bledsoe led his team with 26 points
and notched a triple-double, grabbing 12 boards and dishing out 13 assists. His play was
also crucial in the clutch. After holding a double-digit lead, Sacramento (31-30) finished
the fourth quarter on a quick run and took their first lead since the second quarter to go
up 126-124. Bledsoe, though, was able to tie the game with a layup off the glass before
locking down Sacramento's De'Aaron Fox on the final possession. Milwaukee also saw a gem
from Malcolm Brogdon, who tallied 25 points in the win. Despite playing limited minutes,
Antetokounmpo made a splash, recording 17 points, seven rebounds, and two blocks. The
Kings, meanwhile, saw 32 points from star shooting guard Buddy Hield. Bogdan Bogdanovic
added another 28 points, which he coupled with eight rebounds and eight assists. Fox was
