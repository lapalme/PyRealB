The Denver Nuggets defeated the visiting Houston Rockets, 136-122, at Pepsi Center on
Friday evening. The Rockets got out to a strong start on the road, as they outscored the
Nuggets 43-35 in the first quarter. However, the Nuggets responded by outscoring the
Rockets by 20 in the second quarter, to take an 83-71 lead into halftime. The Nuggets
maintained a double-digit lead through most of the third quarter, and built their lead to
18 by the start of the fourth. The Rockets kept battling through the fourth, but it was
not enough, as the Nuggets cruised to their fifth straight win. Nikola Jokic came up huge
for the Nuggets, with 31 points on 12-of-15 shooting, 13 rebounds, nine assists and one
steal, in 31 minutes. Malik Beasley finished with 35 points on 12-of-17 shooting, three
assists, two rebounds and one steal, while Torrey Craig added 22 points, four rebounds,
two assists, a steal and a block. The Nuggets shot 62 percent from the field, including
14-of-31 from long range. Meanwhile, James Harden led the Rockets with 30 points on
9-of-21 shooting, nine assists, six rebounds, two steals and one block, in 37 minutes.
Chris Paul tallied 20 points, six assists and three rebounds, in 29 minutes, while Kenneth
Faried added 23 points, six rebounds and one assist. The Rockets shot 53 percent from the
field, including 16-of-44 from long range. The Rockets (29-22) will look to bounce back as
they take on the Jazz in Utah, on Saturday. The Nuggets (36-15) take on the Timberwolves
in Minnesota on Saturday.
