The Chicago Bulls (15-8) defeated the Miami Heat (11-13) 93-75 on Sunday. Chicago has won
four of their last five games. Mike Dunleavy took a rare lead on offense Sunday, finishing

start. Gibson finished with 15 points and nine rebounds in 38 minutes. The Bulls will
continue their two-game road trip in Atlanta on Monday against the Hawks. Miami has lost
six of their last eight games, and Sunday they were without both Chris Bosh (calf) and

without their starting frontcourt. With Bosh out, the Heat struggled to get anything
working on offense and shot a woeful 35 percent from the field and 18 percent from beyond
the arc. Miami will go on the road Tuesday to face the Brooklyn Nets.
