*** Reference summary
The Brooklyn Nets defeated the Chicago Bulls, 107-106, at Barclays Center on Saturday
afternoon. Someone apparently forgot to tell the Nets (20-60) that they have nothing left
to play for, as they've been particularly impressive in the second half of the season. One
of their recent trademarks has been giving quality teams a very difficult time, even those
with plenty of motivation for playoff positioning. The Bulls (39-41) were the latest team
to learn that lesson the hard way, as they fell victim to a Brooklyn fourth-quarter
comeback and dropped a one-point decision that could jeopardize their playoff hopes.
Spencer Dinwiddie's two free throws with 13 seconds remaining snapped a 103-103 tie, and
Jimmy Butler's three-pointer with one second left came too little too late for Chicago.
While the Bulls shot better than the Nets and also won the rebounding battle by a 52-44
margin, they also committed 17 turnovers, contributing to their demise. Butler led the way
for the visitors with 33 points, seven rebounds and four assists. Robin Lopez tallied 16
points, eight rebounds and one block. Jerian Grant followed with 15 points, five assists,
three rebounds, one steal and one block. Dwyane Wade made his return from an elbow injury
that was once thought to be season-ending and posted 14 points, seven rebounds, two
assists and a block over 25 minutes. Nikola Mirotic rounded out the starting five's night
with four points, seven rebounds, two steals and two blocks. Caris LeVert and Dinwiddie
led the way for Brooklyn with matching 19-point efforts. LeVert added five rebounds, one
assist and one steal while Dinwiddie supplied four assists, three rebounds and two steals.
Rondae Hollis-Jefferson generated a 16-point, 12-rebound double-double that also included
three assists, three steals and one block. Brook Lopez tallied 13 points, four rebounds,
one assist and one block. Jeremy Lin filled out the stat sheet with 12 points, seven
assists, six rebounds and three steals. The Bulls head back home to face the Orlando Magic
on Monday night, while the Nets head to Beantown to tangle with the Boston Celtics on
Monday evening as well.
